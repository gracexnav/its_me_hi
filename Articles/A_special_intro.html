A Special Introduction

We have talked about a number of things. But we have yet to introduce ourselves. So we decided to give an intro to each other this week.

I first saw Jill when I came as a new admission to class. She was the star of the class. At first I thought we wouldn't be best friends. But we became close over the years. The one character about Jill is that she has a strong will. She is the strongest person I know. We both love solving things. I remember in 7th grade our math teacher would ask students to solve problems on board. And we both were first to volunteer. Our teacher would be like not you guys again. But no one in the class would come forward to solve it except me, my sister and of course Jill. We had our fights and differences as friends usually do, but it made us closer. I grew over the years as a very good person (I think I am a good person) thanks to my friends. Jill would be the logical person of our friend group. But she does like to pick funny fights with another friend in the group. We would all say, 'not another fight'. She is always ready to do new things, even if they are crazy. I remember falling for reading and asking her to try reading, and she did. We would have fun discussing books. She loves her family. She loves solving problems. She loves trying new things. You may think we were complete nerds, but it is only partly true. Of course, we would have lengthy conversations about why dividing by zero will give infinity. But on the other hand we were mischievous.

I remember several funny incidents. Pranking our friends was a daily routine. One day while getting our dairy sign from our math teacher (because our class teacher was absent) I gave my diary to Jill to get it signed for me too. And he was like 'I will sign only when she comes to get it'. Jill came to give the diary to me and suggested not getting a sign. I just put it in my bag without getting a sign. We both gave him a very hard time before getting it signed. Jill was covering for me. We both had tons of fun that day. Finally when I went to get a sign, he put an autograph saying, 'Since you guys gave me a hard time, I will give you an autograph'. She is funny, loyal and compassionate. She is straight forward. We were competitive. We don't back down from a fight. I always admired Jill because she is strong, smart and humble. She is Jill. How can I not like her?

She always voices her opinion strongly and clearly. We would always push her to join oratory competitions and debate. When I was having a hard time, she made me feel ok, and I didn't hate studying when I was reading with her. I was always inspired to be strong like her. I had a hard time learning after she switched schools. Learning was not so fun without her. So I had a journey to enjoy learning in my way. She was the sunlight that pushed me to grow a lot. This week's blog may feel boring to others, but it means a lot to me. I would like to thank Jill for being my friend over the years. Of course, our friendship changed over the years, but it is still there as a constant source of happiness for me. Jill, you are amazing. Be yourself. If I can choose a person who will never give up on anything, it would be her.
